Chihiro Shibayama is a versatile freelance percussionist and educator whose career spans Broadway, opera, symphony, new music, and television — shaped by over two decades of performing and teaching at the highest levels of the profession.

Ms. Shibayama was a percussionist for the successful Broadway run of Miss Saigon (March 2017 – January 2018), and made her Broadway debut as one of three on-stage musicians for The Cherry Orchard starring Diane Lane, produced by The Roundabout Theatre Company at the American Airlines Theatre. She has performed for the Radio City Christmas Spectacular, NBC's Good Morning America with John Legend and Common, the Daytime Emmy Awards, and Broadway productions including The Addams Family, Anything Goes, Cinderella, and West Side Story.

Opera & Orchestra

Among her most notable performances, Ms. Shibayama was one of three on-stage percussion soloists in the U.S. premiere of Tan Dun's opera TEA – A Mirror of Soul at The Santa Fe Opera (2007), the East Coast premiere at the Opera Company of Philadelphia (2009), and the Canadian premiere at the Vancouver Opera (2013). She has performed with The Metropolitan Opera, Kansas City Symphony, San Diego Symphony, New Jersey Symphony, Huntsville Symphony Orchestra, the Orchestra Now (TON), and many others. As a soloist, she has performed concerti with the Great Lake Symphony Orchestra and Manitou Music Festival Orchestra.

New Music & Chamber

As a passionate new music advocate, Ms. Shibayama has performed and recorded with Alarm Will Sound, American Contemporary Music Ensemble (ACME), S.E.M. Ensemble, Second Instrumental Unit, New Juilliard Ensemble, and AXIOM. She toured China in 2015 to perform at the Beijing Modern Music Festival, and has appeared on the Amazon Prime series Mozart in the Jungle. She is a co-founder and program coordinator of Multicultural Sonic Evolution (MuSE), a non-profit creating multicultural environments through music, dance, theater, and film.

Teaching & Education

Ms. Shibayama has dedicated more than 20 years to percussion education. She served as percussion faculty at Diller-Quaile School of Music for seven years and Third Street Music Settlement for two years. From 2007 to 2010 she was faculty at The Juilliard Summer Percussion Seminar, where she coached ensembles, presented masterclasses, and worked with exceptional young musicians from around the world. Her masterclass "My Way to Broadway" has been presented at the University of Missouri, the University of Alabama in Huntsville, and the Juilliard Summer Percussion Masterclass Series.

She regularly collaborates with teaching artists from the New York Philharmonic's Very Young Composers program, introducing young students to the percussion family. She is currently accepting private students in Longmont, Colorado and online worldwide.

Librarian & Production

Beyond performing and teaching, Ms. Shibayama has built an extensive career as a freelance ensemble librarian. She has served as librarian for Alarm Will Sound, Orchestra of St. Luke's, New York Pops, The Juilliard School, and New York Youth Symphony. Since 2015 she has been a percussion specialist and production assistant for Carnegie Hall / Weill Music Institute's National Youth Orchestra of the U.S.A., supporting the orchestra on tours across Asia, Europe, and South America.

Background & Training

A native of Yokohama City, Japan, Ms. Shibayama graduated from the Interlochen Arts Academy with a performance award in 2003. She earned her Bachelor of Music and Master of Music degrees from The Juilliard School in 2007 and 2009. Her mentors include Daniel Druckman, Greg Zuber, Markus Rhoten, Joe Pereira, Roland Kohloff, Keith Aleo, Mariko Okada, and John Alfieri. She proudly endorses Pearl Adams Concert Percussion and Dragonfly Percussion.
